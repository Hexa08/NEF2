## General Questions

### How does NEF2 differ from PyTorch or JAX?
NEF2 is designed as a lower-level "substrate." While PyTorch focuses on researcher ergonomics, NEF2 focuses on machine efficiency and agent-native workflows. We bypass the overhead of traditional frameworks by utilizing a unified DAL and the HyperCache memory hierarchy.

### Can I use my existing PyTorch models?
Yes. NEF2 includes a converter for ONNX and Safetensors formats. You can also use our `nef2.compiler` to capture graphs from standard Python code.

## Installation & Setup

### My GPU isn't being detected. What should I do?
Ensure you have the latest drivers for your hardware. Run the diagnostic tool:
```python
import nef2.gpu as gpu
gpu.diagnostic_report()
```
Check if the required libraries (CUDA, ROCm, or Metal) are in your system's library path.

### Can I run NEF2 without a GPU?
Yes. NEF2 has a high-performance C++/SIMD backend for CPUs. However, many advanced features like HyperCache and large-scale training require a supported accelerator for optimal performance.

## Performance Tuning

### Why is my model training slower than expected?
-   **Check the Bottleneck**: Use the NEF Profiler (`nef2.metrics.profile`) to see if you are CPU-bound, I/O-bound, or GPU-bound.
-   **Enable Compilation**: Ensure you are using `nef2.compile(model)` to enable kernel fusion.
-   **HyperCache Config**: Adjust your `max_hot_tokens` and `max_warm_tokens` settings to better match your VRAM capacity.

### How do I reduce VRAM usage?
Utilize **TurboQuant** to quantize your weights to FP8 or INT8, and ensure **HyperCache** is enabled to page less-frequently used data to system RAM.

## Distributed Training

### What networking hardware is recommended?
For multi-node training, we strongly recommend InfiniBand or 100GbE+ with RoCE support to take full advantage of our RDMA networking stack.
